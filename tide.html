Sovanna Fenty has always belonged to the ocean.

As an ocean engineer, she understands its rhythms, its unpredictability, and the quiet language of tides better than most people understand each other. Warm, talkative, and impossible to ignore, she moves through life like sunlight scattering across water.

Vieste, Italy, is meant to be just another assignment.

Until she meets Theon Wakefield.

Once an Olympic swimmer, Theon was built for water. Now he avoids it. After an accident ended his career, he disappeared from the world that once celebrated him, resurfacing only as a lifeguard in a quiet coastal village that has already decided what kind of man he is: distant, cold, untouchable.

They are wrong.

Because behind the gruff silence and guarded distance, Theon lives a life no one has ever seen. A life filled with hidden softness, from two dogs who follow him like shadows, to birds that perch like secrets, to a hamster he insists nobody is allowed to know about.

Sovanna is the first disruption to his carefully contained world.

She talks too much. She cares too easily. She refuses to be intimidated by silence.

And Theon, who has spent years mastering the art of staying away from people, finds himself unable to look away from her.

In a village where he is the storm and she is the sun, neither expects what happens when warmth begins to reach what was never meant to thaw.

Because some collisions don’t destroy.

They reshape everything they touch.
