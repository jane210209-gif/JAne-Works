For as long as he could remember, Leander and Vesparine had been inseparable.Childhood friends. Partners in crime. The kind of people who knew each other's coffee orders, worst habits, and embarrassing stories without ever needing to ask.

Which was exactly the problem.

Because somewhere between shared study sessions, late-night calls, and years of being the most important person in each other's lives, friendship had quietly become something far more dangerous.

At least for Leander.

Unfortunately, Vesparine remained completely unaware.

Or so he thought.

Now an automobile engineering student trying to survive university, Leander finds himself battling a far greater challenge than exams or deadlines: a relentlessly chaotic friend group that seems determined to turn his private feelings into public entertainment.

To them, Leander's hopeless devotion is obvious.

To Vesparine, it's apparently invisible.

And to Leander himself, every accidental touch, every shoulder nap, every moment she walks into his apartment using the spare key he regrettably gave her feels like another step toward disaster.

Then Vesparine begins to notice things.

The way he watches her.

The way he remembers everything she says.

The way he never seems to look away.

For the first time, she starts asking questions she never thought to ask before.

Questions that might change everything.

Caught between years of friendship, a group of friends who have made betting pools on their relationship status, and feelings neither of them are brave enough to confess, Leander and Vesparine discover that sometimes the hardest person to fall in love with is the one who's been beside you all along.
